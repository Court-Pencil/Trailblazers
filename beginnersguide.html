Beginner's Guide

🌦️Check the weather

British weather is famously unpredictable. Sunshine can turn to sideways rain in minutes, so always check the forecast before you head out.

▪️ Use a reliable source like the Met Office or BBC Weather.
▪️ Pack for all conditions—even if it looks clear, bring waterproofs.

👕What to Wear

Depending on the time of year, you may need more or fewer layers — British summers can be breezy, and spring or autumn hikes often call for extra warmth.

▪️Base layer – Breathable tee or thermal
▪️ Mid layer – Fleece or jumper for warmth
▪️ Outer layer – Waterproof jacket with hood
▪️ Bottoms – Quick-dry trousers or leggings
▪️ Footwear – Waterproof hiking boots with grip
▪️ Socks – Thick hiking socks (wool or synthetic)
▪️ Accessories – Hat, gloves, sunglasses, neck warmer (seasonal)

🎒What to Pack

Your backpack is your mobile comfort zone. Pack light, but smart.

▪️ Water: Bring at least 1.5–2 litres. More on hot days.
▪️ Packed Lunch: Sandwiches, wraps, pasta salad—whatever fuels you.
▪️ Snacks: Trail mix, fruit, flapjacks, or anything joyful and energizing.
▪️Essentials: Sunscreen and lip balm
